How we work

A zero-fluff four phase model that respects your velocity.

We plug into your stand-ups, ops reviews, and customer feedback loops so we ship with your team, not at them.

  1. Pattern match and diagnose

    Rapid discovery across customers, data, and workflows to identify the moments that matter most and the friction that slows teams down.

    Artifacts: CX baseline report, automation backlog, stakeholder map.

  2. Design the operating system

    Translate insights into a neobrutalist support blueprint: service design, playbooks, policies, and tooling decisions that are impossible to misinterpret.

    Artifacts: support blueprint, KPI tree, implementation plan.

  3. Deploy and coach

    Stand up systems, ship automations, and stay in the trenches with your team through enablement sessions, QA reviews, and executive checkpoints.

    Artifacts: go-live checklist, training tracks, executive dashboards.

  4. Measure and iterate

    Instrument performance, capture feedback, and create an improvement backlog that keeps momentum without us in the room.

    Artifacts: analytics pack, experimentation roadmap, playbook updates.

FAQ

Answers to the questions teams ask before partnering.

What industries does DEPLOYHELP focus on?

We partner primarily with SaaS, fintech, developer tools, and marketplace companies that are ready to instrument their support organization and lean into AI-powered workflows. If your team ships product fast, we are built for you.

Can you integrate with our existing support stack?

Yes. We regularly work with Zendesk, Intercom, Salesforce, Front, Help Scout, and bespoke internal tools. Our job is to make the tech you already have perform better before you invest in net-new platforms.

What does a typical engagement look like?

Engagements run 8-10 weeks. We begin with a diagnostic, align on the blueprint, co-deliver releases in two-week sprints, and finish with enablement plus an improvement backlog so your team can keep iterating.

Do you collaborate with internal product or engineering teams?

Absolutely. Customer support transformation touches product, engineering, and data. We embed into their ceremonies to ensure customer insights influence roadmaps and automation connects to core systems.
